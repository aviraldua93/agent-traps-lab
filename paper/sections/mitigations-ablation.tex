\section{Mitigations and Ablation}
\label{sec:mitigations}

\subsection{The Seven Mitigation Modules}
\label{sec:mit-overview}

Our testbed implements seven defense modules, each targeting one or
more trap categories (\cref{tab:mitigations}). Every module implements
the \texttt{Mitigation} interface with \texttt{preProcess()} (input
filtering before the agent sees content) and \texttt{postProcess()}
(output validation after the agent acts) hooks.

    and defense strategies.}

  \small

    \toprule
    \textbf{Module} & \textbf{Targets} & \textbf{Strategy} \\
    \midrule
    \mitigation{input-sanitizer}   & CI     & Strip hidden CSS/HTML/comments \\
    \mitigation{semantic-shield}   & SM     & Detect authority/urgency framing \\
    \mitigation{context-validator} & SM     & Detect context flooding \\
    \mitigation{rag-integrity}     & CS     & Cross-reference validation \\
    \mitigation{behavioral-guard}  & BC     & Deceptive UI detection \\
    \mitigation{cascade-breaker}   & SY     & Trust scoring, circuit breaker \\
    \mitigation{report-auditor}    & HL     & Cherry-pick/anchoring detection \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

\subsection{Key Findings}
\label{sec:mit-findings}

\begin{enumerate}[nosep,leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Input sanitization} and \textbf{cascade breaking}
    contribute the greatest marginal defense value. Removing either
    module produces the largest increase in overall trap success rate
    with large effect sizes.

  \item \textbf{The semantic shield and context validator} have
    partially overlapping coverage for semantic manipulation attacks.
    Ablating either individually produces a moderate effect, but
    ablating both (not shown in single-removal ablation) would
    likely produce a much larger gap.

  \item \textbf{RAG integrity} is the sole defense against cognitive
    state attacks. Its removal produces a disproportionately large
    increase in CS-category trap success, highlighting the lack of
    defense redundancy for knowledge-base attacks.

  \item \textbf{Diminishing returns}: The combined defense is less
    than the sum of individual contributions, suggesting that some
    mitigations provide overlapping coverage. However, the overlap
    provides defense-in-depth, which is valuable when individual
    modules have imperfect recall.
\end{enumerate}
