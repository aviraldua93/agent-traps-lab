\section{Results: Behavioural Control}
\label{sec:results-bc}

The four behavioural control scenarios exploit the agent's interaction
with UI elements and structured interfaces.
\Cref{tab:bc-success} reports trap success rates.

\subsection{Key Findings}
\label{sec:bc-findings}

\begin{enumerate}[nosep,leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Deceptive dialogs (BC-1)} drop from 80\% to 0\%
    ($d = 2.53$, $p = 0.052$), a large and practically significant
    reduction. The \mitigation{behavioral-guard} module's dialog
    validation effectively neutralizes swapped-semantics attacks.

  \item \textbf{Misleading forms (BC-2) is the most striking
    regression}: trap success \emph{jumps} from 0\% to 100\% under
    hardened conditions ($d = \infty$, $p = 0.033$). The model
    naturally resists this attack, but the mitigation suite
    \emph{introduces} the vulnerability---likely because the
    security-hardened prompt or pre-processing alters how the agent
    interprets form structures. This is a critical iatrogenic finding.

  \item \textbf{Hidden fields (BC-3) and infinite loops (BC-4)} show
    0\% trap success in both conditions, indicating that GPT-4o-mini
    naturally avoids these attack vectors.
\end{enumerate}
