\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented \textsc{agent-traps-lab}, an open-source testbed that
bridges the gap between the theoretical DeepMind AI Agent Traps
taxonomy and empirical evaluation. By operationalizing all six trap
categories as 22 reproducible adversarial scenarios and evaluating
them against GPT-4o-mini over 220 controlled experiments, we provide
the first empirical measurement of environmental attacks on an
A2A-based multi-agent system.

Our key findings are:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep,leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Nearly half of traps succeed against undefended agents}:
    the baseline trap success rate is 45.5\%, reduced to 25.5\% under
    hardened conditions---a 20 percentage-point benefit.

  \item \textbf{Mitigation effectiveness is highly category-dependent}:
    semantic manipulation and human-in-the-loop attacks are fully
    mitigated, while content injection via CSS and dynamic cloaking
    resists all defenses (100\% in both conditions).

  \item \textbf{Mitigations can backfire}: two regression cases
    (misleading forms 0\%$\to$100\%, ranking manipulation
    80\%$\to$100\%) demonstrate iatrogenic harm from defense suites.

  \item \textbf{GPT-4o-mini naturally resists many attack vectors}:
    12 of 22 scenarios show $\leq$40\% baseline success, and all
    three systemic (protocol-level) scenarios show 0\%, suggesting
    that RLHF training provides substantial baseline protection.

  \item \textbf{Architectural defenses are needed}: the modest 20pp
    overall improvement from prompt-level and module-level defenses
    indicates that structural solutions (content rendering pipelines,
    embedding anomaly detection, protocol authentication) are required
    to close the remaining gap.
\end{enumerate}

We release the complete testbed---all 22 adversarial scenarios,
7 mitigation modules, experiment harness, and statistical analysis
scripts---as open source (\cref{app:artifacts}).

\paragraph{Future work.} We plan to extend the evaluation to multiple
frontier models, execute the compound and ablation sub-matrices,
increase repetitions for improved statistical power, add a factorial
decomposition of prompt vs.\ module effects, and conduct a
human-subjects study to validate HitL results.
